Caspi et al. (2003)

  • 2025-06-04

Aim

To investigate the link between alleles of 5-HTT gene and Major Depressive Disorder (MDD).

Participants

Opportunity sample of 847 participants, split into three groups depending on their lengths of the alleles on their 5-HTT gene.

Procedures

  1. The participants were asked to report any stressful life events that had taken place between their 21st birthday and 26th birthday.

  2. Diagnostic Interview Schedule was used to assess incidences of depression.

  3. Correlational analysis was carried out between co-variables:

  • Each participant’s stressful life events and incidences of depression

  • Length of each participant’s alleles and incidences of depression

  • Perceived stress and the length of each participant’s alleles

Results

  1. Highest number of depressive episodes in response to stressful life events was reported by participants who had two short 5-HTT alleles.

  2. Participants with two long alleles reported fewer symptoms overall.

Conclusions

  1. Correlation between short 5-HTT alleles and prevalence of MDD.

  2. Serotonin level may be a factor of MDD.

  3. Onset of MDD appears to be an interaction between environment and genetic make-up.

Evaluation

  1. Provides application of genetic screening for MDD treatment.

  2. Each measure is checked by the findings of other measures, higher internal validity.

  3. Self-report is prone to social desirability bias and response bias.

  4. Oversimplistic and reductionist.
